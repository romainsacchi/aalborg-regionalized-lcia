# Instructor Notes

These notes translate participant feedback into a consistent facilitation pattern for the Aalborg course. The objective is to preserve the depth of the notebooks while giving participants protected time to think, write code, compare approaches, and explain results.

## Core Teaching Loop

Use the same sequence for each major concept:

1. **Orient (2-3 minutes):** state the question, expected output, and relevant notebook cells.
2. **Demonstrate (10-15 minutes):** explain one small example and the reason behind the implementation.
3. **Attempt (10-20 minutes):** participants work without opening hidden solution cells.
4. **Pair-check (5 minutes):** participants compare code and interpretations with a neighbour.
5. **Debrief (5-10 minutes):** discuss different approaches, common errors, and the meaning of the result.

Announce the deliverable and the available time before each attempt. Do not live-code the answer during protected work periods.

## Two-Instructor Roles

For each block, assign one **lead instructor** and one **roaming instructor**.

- The lead instructor explains the concept, keeps time, and runs the debrief.
- The roaming instructor checks progress, gives the smallest useful hint, and records recurring blockers.
- Swap roles between blocks.
- If several teams hit the same blocker, pause once for a short whole-room clarification instead of repeating the same explanation.

## Checkpoint Guidance

- Keep solution cells hidden until the debrief.
- Ask participants to predict the result before running code when practical.
- Prefer hints that identify the next object, method, or diagnostic step; avoid taking over the keyboard.
- Ask early finishers to change one assumption, activity, location, or method and explain what changes.
- During the debrief, ask at least one team to explain its reasoning and another to identify a limitation.
- Capture unresolved technical questions for the final ten minutes of the block.

## Core Versus Optional Material

Follow the course route stated near the start of each long uncertainty notebook.

- In `D1-05`, sections 1-7 are core; sections 8-9 are optional extensions.
- In `D2-06`, sections 1-7 are core; section 8 is an optional extension.

Optional sections are useful for fast finishers and post-course study. Do not compress protected exercises to cover them in the live session.

## Capstone Facilitation

- Form teams of three or four.
- Assign a driver, navigator, reviewer, and reporter; in teams of three, combine reviewer and reporter.
- Let teams choose their assumptions and comparison before offering implementation advice.
- At the midpoint, check that every team has at least one reproducible result and one interpretation.
- Keep reports to five minutes, followed by one peer question.
- Evaluate reasoning, transparency, and interpretation rather than the size or complexity of the result.
